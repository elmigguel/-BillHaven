## THE BOTTOM LINE

### NO-KYC MODEL = NOT VIABLE ❌

**BillHaven CANNOT legally operate without KYC in Netherlands/EU.**

**Why:**
- MiCA (EU regulation) effective Dec 30, 2024 - requires KYC for ALL crypto transactions
- Dutch WWFT (AML law) - mandatory identity verification
- No transaction thresholds (€1,000 rule is a myth - applies only to ADDITIONAL verification)
- "Online shop" argument legally invalid (escrow = regulated financial service)
- P2P exemption requires FULL decentralization (no company, no interface, no revenue)

**Enforcement:**
- Bybit fined €2.25M (Oct 2024) for operating without license
- DNB/AFM actively prosecuting unlicensed operators
- Criminal penalties + administrative fines

---

## THE VIABLE PATH: FULL LICENSING ✅

### What BillHaven Needs:

**1. CASP License (Crypto Asset Service Provider)**
- Regulator: AFM (Authority for Financial Markets)
- Timeline: 5-8 months application process
- Cost: €10,000-€100,000 application fee
- Capital: €125,000-€150,000 minimum required

**2. Payment Institution License (Likely Required)**
- Regulator: DNB (De Nederlandsche Bank)
- Reason: Escrow services = payment service under PSD2
- Example: Vistra got PSD2 license specifically for escrow (2022)
- Timeline: 3-6 months
- Cost: €15,000-€50,000 additional

**3. Full KYC/AML Compliance**
- Identity verification for ALL users
- Sanctions screening (OFAC, EU lists)
- Transaction monitoring
- Suspicious Activity Reporting
- 5-7 year record keeping
